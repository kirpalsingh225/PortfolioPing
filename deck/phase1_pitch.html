Problem and Usefulness

Portfolio tracking should not require learning a complex dashboard.

PortfolioPing · AI Builders Hackathon

Investing apps can overwhelm new users

  • Holdings, positions, watchlists, alerts, and news live across different screens.
  • Charts, tabs, and financial terms create friction for beginner and non-technical investors.
  • Simple checks often require repeatedly opening broker apps and navigating dashboards.
  • Users want quick answers without receiving risky investment advice or placing accidental trades.
Value proposition: from complex dashboards to simple conversations on WhatsApp.

Impact and Roadmap

A working MVP for simpler, safer portfolio monitoring.

Core flow ready for final demo
Who it helps

Beginner and non-technical retail investors who find dashboard-heavy investing apps difficult to navigate.

Useful today

Portfolio summary, holdings, positions, watchlists, alert creation, sourced market search, and contextual follow-ups.

Why it is different

No new dashboard to learn. Users access core portfolio workflows through an app they already use daily.

Proof from the deployed MVP
  • Live WhatsApp onboarding and secure Zerodha connect flow.
  • Recent context handles “set an alert for it” and “remove the same stock”.
  • GitHub Actions checks saved alerts every 10 minutes.
Next milestones
  • Enable Zerodha quote/LTP permission for live alert delivery.
  • Add multi-stock and multi-intent requests in one message.
  • Onboard a dedicated WhatsApp Business number for public users.
Positioning: PortfolioPing is a conversational portfolio-monitoring layer, not an investment-advisory platform.
